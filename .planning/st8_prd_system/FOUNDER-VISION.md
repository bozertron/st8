## The Core Insight: Where Products Die

> "Complex hardware/software products go off the rails in the **all-hands kickoff meeting**.
> Why? Because you have a room full of people speaking **different domain languages** about something that **doesn't exist yet**."
>
> — Founder

### The Solution: Make the Product REAL Before the Meeting

The PRD system must generate a **PRD PACKAGE** — a collection of documents and assets so compelling that every stakeholder sees their role reflected in a tangible, exciting future product.

**Before kickoff, every department head should be able to look at the PRD package and say:**
- *"I know exactly what chipset I'm targeting."* — Electrical Engineer
- *"I know where to focus my acoustic rendering work."* — Firmware Engineer
- *"I know the margin target and the channel strategy."* — CFO/CRO
- *"I know the buying groups and launch regions."* — CMO
- *"I know the price point and competitive positioning."* — Sales Director
- *"I know the manufacturing tolerances and certification requirements."* — Mechanical Engineer
- *"I know the tooling and production timeline."* — Manufacturing/Tooling Engineer
- *"I know the go-to-market narrative and press angles."* — Communications/GTM Specialist

## The PRD Package: What's Included

The PRD is **never a single document**. It's a **multimedia package** that makes the product feel real:

### 1. Product Definition
- Technical specifications (software + hardware)
- Architecture diagrams
- Component breakdowns
- BOM (Bill of Materials) with target costs
- Manufacturing process overview

### 2. Market & Business Documents
- **Press release** (launch announcement)
- **Marketing pitch decks**
- **Sales channel strategy**
- **Go-to-market (GTM) plan**
- **Pricing strategy**
- **Margin analysis** (2x CoGS targets, etc.)
- **Financial projections** (revenue, stock price impact)

### 3. Internal Alignment Documents
- **Internal communications** (how the product is represented in every region)
- **Stakeholder aspiration summary** (what each person wants)
- **Cross-department opportunity map** (hidden alignments discovered)
- **Workback schedule** (timeline from now to launch)

### 4. External-Facing Materials
- **Distribution partner briefs**
- **Retailer presentation materials**
- **End-customer communications**
- **Technology partnership announcements**
- **Engineering award submissions**
- **Influencer/reviewer briefs**

### 5. Experience & Branding
- **Product images / renders** (if available)
- **Brand voice & tone guide**
- **Regional customization notes**
- **Launch event concepts**

## The Stakeholder Input Loop

The PRD system is not a document generator — it's a **conversational alignment engine**.

### Phase 1: Stakeholder Discovery
1. **Product Owner** identifies all individuals who will feed into the process
2. System captures data on their role via:
   - Internal records
   - Input from the Product Owner
   - Direct conversation with the stakeholder

### Phase 2: Conversational Capture (One by One)
- Each stakeholder engages in a **voice-first conversation** with the system
- The system asks **leading questions** designed to:
  - Surface what they **love** and **want**
  - Reveal their **aspirations** and **constraints**
  - Leave **breadcrumbs** that connect to realities in other departments
- Natural language input — no forms, no sterile surveys

### Phase 3: Opportunity Synthesis
- System **cross-references** all stakeholder inputs
- Identifies **hidden alignments** (e.g., firmware engineer's acoustic rendering + product manager's auto-pairing headphones + sales director's $799-$1499 gap = flagship headphones at $1299)
- Flags **tensions** and **open questions** that need resolution

### Phase 4: PRD Package Draft
- System generates the complete PRD package
- Every stakeholder's input is reflected
- The package makes the product **feel real**

### Phase 5: Objection Cycle
- Each stakeholder reviews their sections
- System captures **objections** and **suggestions**
- Updates the draft
- **Repeats until everyone is on the same page**

### Phase 6: Lock for Execution
- PRD package is **frozen**
- Becomes the execution blueprint
- Maps to st8 lifecycle phases: LOCKED → WIRING → DEVELOPMENT → PRODUCTION

## Voice-First Interaction Model

> "I think people would much prefer to have a conversation."
>
> — Founder

### Design Principles:
- **Conversational, not transactional** — No forms, no drop-downs, no sterile fields
- **Voice input support** — Speak naturally, system listens and parses
- **Leading questions** — System guides the conversation toward actionable insights
- **Personality** — The system has warmth, curiosity, and enthusiasm for the product
- **Adaptive** — Questions evolve based on previous answers and department context

### Example Conversation Flow:

**System:** "Tell me about a product or feature you've always wanted to build but never had the chance."

**Electrical Engineer:** "I've been dying to deploy this new chipset with integrated neural audio processing. It would let us do real-time acoustic scene rendering."

**System:** *{notes: acoustic scene rendering, neural audio, real-time processing}*

**System:** "What kind of product would really show that off?"

**EE:** "Premium headphones, definitely. But they'd need to be smart enough to switch contexts automatically — like from TV to car to phone."

**System:** *{notes: auto-pairing, context switching, TV/car/phone, premium positioning}*

**System:** "What price range do you think would make this a flagship product?"

**EE:** "If it's truly premium... $1299? That's where the serious audiophile market is."

**System:** *{notes: target price $1299, audiophile market}*

→ Later, when talking to the Sales Director:

**System:** "We're looking at a premium headphone product in the $1299 range. Is that a gap you could fill in your channel strategy?"

**Sales Director:** "Actually yes — we've been dying to get something premium in the $799-$1499 range. Our best relationships are with [Buying Group], Costco, and Best Buy."

**System:** *{cross-reference: $1299 matches target range, Buying Group/Costco/Best Buy are channels, local market promotions align with buying group preferences}*

→ Opportunity synthesis:
- **Product:** Premium smart headphones, $1299
- **Key feature:** Auto-pairing between TV/car/phone with acoustic scene rendering
- **Target channels:** Buying Group, Costco, Best Buy
- **Promotion strategy:** Local market promotions (buying group preference)
- **Partnerships:** Auto dealers, custom home builders (for TV/car integration)
- **Events:** Regional hi-fi clubs (show off tech)
- **Engineering focus:** Neural audio chipset, real-time acoustic rendering
- **Firmware focus:** Context-switching algorithms

## The "Fun" Mandate

> "I want this to be an intimate and enjoyable experience. NOT sterile.
> We can yield sterile procurement, RFQ, etc. reports from it.
> This is for people who are passionate about their work."
>
> — Founder

### Design Implications:
- **Tone:** Warm, enthusiastic, genuinely curious about the user's work
- **Visuals:** Rich, engaging, multimedia — not dry text documents
- **Interaction:** Conversational, with moments of delight and surprise
- **Progress:** Visible, celebratory — "Look how much we've discovered together!"
- **Output:** Beautiful, shareable documents that people are **proud** to present

### Anti-Patterns (What We Avoid):
- ❌ Cold, clinical interfaces
- ❌ Long forms with mandatory fields
- ❌ Generic templates that feel like Mad Libs
- ❌ Output that looks like a government procurement document
- ❌ One-size-fits-all questioning

## Technology Stack Alignment

### Database:
- **SQLite** (primary)
- **JSON backups** in project directory
- **Markdown exports** in project directory

### LLM Integration:
- **Tap st8's existing LLM infrastructure** — no new provider integration needed
- Use existing entity configuration patterns

### Voice Input:
- Browser-native Web Speech API for MVP
- Upgrade path to Whisper or similar for production
- Real-time transcription with intent parsing

### Output Formats:
- **Markdown** (primary, for version control and diffs)
- **JSON** (structured data, API consumption)
- **DOCX/PDF** (stakeholder handoffs)
- **HTML** (interactive web views)
- **Images** (renders, diagrams — referenced, not generated by this system)

## Open Design Questions for the Team

1. **Voice conversation flow:** How do we design questions that feel natural but consistently extract structured data?
2. **Cross-department opportunity detection:** What NLP/LLM techniques best identify hidden alignments across stakeholder inputs?
3. **Objection cycle UX:** How do we make "raising concerns" feel constructive rather than adversarial?
4. **PRD package aesthetics:** What templates make technical specs AND marketing materials equally beautiful?
5. **Stakeholder identity:** How does the system know who to interview and when? (Product Owner-driven vs. auto-discovery?)
6. **Progressive disclosure:** How do we avoid overwhelming stakeholders with "the whole package" on first review?
7. **Cultural adaptation:** How does tone/adapt to different industries (industrial equipment vs. premium consumer products)?
