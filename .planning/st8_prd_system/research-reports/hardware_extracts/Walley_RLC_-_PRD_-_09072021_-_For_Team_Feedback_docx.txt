Market Need
T
he world is overhauling their electrical grids to service contemporary needs. There are no exceptions.
Underdeveloped countries are targeting state of the art infrastructure in favor of legacy designs
, developed countries are investing in comprehensive redesigns.
There is a large emerging market for next generation power generation, storage, and management. The usual suspects in this market would be alternative energy generators that have been heavily marketed globally
– solar panels, wind turbines,
etc..
And c
ompanies like Tesla who are
leading the charge with devices like the
powerwall
for dedicated storge,
and
grid/local switching.
There are more companies, but this is the ‘mass
perspective’
. The public has yet to gain a ‘
Ford
vs.
Chevy’ level of understanding in this market, the lack of popular opinion will be leveraged in the product’s favor.
At a professional – infrastructure grade – level, there are
plenty of devices that will estimate consumption, but when it comes to taking action, the only options are effectively toys built for weekend warriors
– time consuming to install, a pain to configure, and ill-defined form factors
, or
,
expensive - $1000+ per circuit c
ontactors
+
PLC’s

There is no middle ground product that provides the masses a safe, reliable, method to perform a granular power consumption analysis, and then dynamically allocate power based on the observations
and conditions
,
while
also be
ing
install friendly – in turn making it economically viable – to the
professionals who are installing it at scale.
That is

Walley is the middle ground product that is designed by professionals, for professionals
– to enable

scale
.
Walley will provide professionals a new revenue opportunity, while helping governments and utilities manage power

Walley is compatible in
a wide variety of AC
Power Grids
:

Walley can meet the building and safety code for a 20A device in a 120V AC grid (Canada/USA):

Single Power Outlet Mode – Requirements:

Control OUT1 and OUT2 in parallel
at all times

Provide power monitor feedback for each output.
Single 2-way dimmer mode (both outputs operate in parallel, variable from 0% to 100% PWM) – Requirements:
Present to the RF as a single 2-way dimmer with power monitoring capability and an additional power monitoring device.

at all times

Provide power monitor feedback for dimmer device (OUT1).
Provide power monitor feedback for power monitor device (OUT2).

Present to the RF as two 2-way dimmers (

Respond to CTRL2 by adjusting OUT2 PWM to be proportional to the voltage seen on CTRL2 (0V = 0%, 1.5V = 50%, 3V = 100%).
Provide power monitor feedback for each dimmer device.
Single 2-way switch (output 1) and a single 2-way dimmer (output 2, variable from 0% to 100% PWM) – Requirements:
Present to the RF as a single 2-way switch and as a single 2-way dimmer (SWITCH 1 and DIMMER 2) each with power monitoring capability.
Control OUT1 and OUT2 independently.
Respond to RF control.
Respond to CRTL1 by toggling OUT1 when CTRL1 is toggled (2-way switch).
Respond to CTRL2 by adjusting OUT2 PWM to be proportional to the voltage seen on CTRL2 (0V = 0%, 1.5V = 50%, 3V = 100%).
Provide power monitor feedback for the switch and dimmer independently.

Provide power monitor feedback for the 3-way dimmer for OUT1.
